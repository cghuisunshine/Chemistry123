13.16 Vibrational Raman spectra of polyatomic molecules

Key ideas

  • The normal modes of vibration of molecules are Raman active if they are accom- panied by a changing polarizability.
  • It is sometimes quite diﬃcult to judge by inspec- tion when this is so.
  • The symmetric stretch of CO2, for example, alternately swells and contracts the molecule: this motion changes the polarizability of the molecule, so the mode is Raman active.
  • The other modes of CO2 leave the polarizability unchanged, so they are Raman inactive.
  • (A mode may be inactive in both.) Because it is often possible to judge intuitively if a I mode changes the molecular dipole moment, we can use this rule to identify modes that are not Raman active.
