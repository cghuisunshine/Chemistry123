14.6 Applications of lasers in chemistry

Key ideas

  • What follows is only an initial listing of applications of lasers to chemistry.
  • We see throughout the text how lasers are also used in the study of macromolecules (Chapter 19) and reaction dynamics (Chapter 24).
  • One application of multiphoton processes is that states inaccess- ible by conventional one-photon spectroscopy become observable because the overall transition occurs with no change of parity.
  • For example, in one-photon spectroscopy, only g ↔ u transitions are observable; in two-photon spectroscopy, however, the over- all outcome of absorbing two photons is a g → g or a u → u transition.
  • The monochromaticity of laser radiation is also a great advantage, for it makes possible the observation of scattered light that diﬀers by only fractions of reciprocal centimetres from the incident radiation.
