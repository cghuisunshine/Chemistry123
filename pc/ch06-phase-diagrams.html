6.1 Definitions

Key ideas

  • A solution of sodium chloride in water is a single phase.
  • Ice is a single phase (P = 1) even though it might be chipped into small fragments.
  • A slurry of ice and water is a two-phase system (P = 2) even though it is diﬃcult to map the boundaries between the phases.
  • An alloy of two metals is a two-phase system (P = 2) if the metals are immiscible, but a single-phase system (P = 1) if they are miscible.
