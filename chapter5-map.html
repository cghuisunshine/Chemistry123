1. Energy, Heat, Work, and the First Law

How energy flows between system and surroundings.

The system is the part of the universe you are studying (for example, reactants in a beaker). The surroundings are everything else (beaker, lab air, etc.).

Heat (q) is energy transfer due to a temperature difference; work (w) is energy transfer when a force moves an object (e.g., gas expanding against a piston).

Sign conventions (chemistry):

  • q > 0: heat flows into the system (endothermic).
  • q < 0: heat flows out of the system (exothermic).
  • w > 0: work done on the system.
  • w < 0: work done by the system.
