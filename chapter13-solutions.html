Chapter 13: Properties of Solutions

Learning Objectives

By the end of this tutorial, you should be able to:

  • Describe factors that affect solubility and rate of dissolution.
  • Express solution concentration in common units (molarity, mass percent, mole fraction).
  • Apply colligative properties (boiling‑point elevation, freezing‑point depression, osmotic pressure).

Key Vocabulary

  • Solution: homogeneous mixture of solute in solvent.
  • Solubility: maximum amount of solute that dissolves in a given amount of solvent at a given T.
  • Saturated solution: contains maximum dissolved solute in equilibrium with undissolved solute.
  • Unsaturated solution: less solute than the saturation limit.
  • Supersaturated solution: contains more solute than predicted by solubility; unstable.
  • Colligative property: depends only on number of solute particles, not their identity.

1. Solubility Factors and Concentration Units

  • “Like dissolves like”: polar solvents dissolve polar/ionic solutes; nonpolar dissolves nonpolar.
  • Solubility of solids usually increases with temperature; gases become less soluble as T increases.
  • Molarity: M = n / V; mass percent, mole fraction, and molality are also common.

2. Colligative Properties

  • Boiling‑point elevation: ΔT_b = i K_b m.
  • Freezing‑point depression: ΔT_f = i K_f m.
  • Osmotic pressure: π = iMRT.
  • i is the van ’t Hoff factor (number of particles per formula unit in solution, ideally).

3. Practice Problems (Self-Check)

  1. Explain why NaCl dissolves readily in water but not in hexane (C6H14).
  2. Calculate the molarity of a solution made by dissolving 5.00 g of KCl in enough water to make 250.0 mL of solution.
  3. A 0.500 m aqueous solution of a non‑electrolyte has Kf = 1.86 °C·kg·mol−1. What is its freezing point?
  4. Compare the freezing‑point depression of 0.10 m NaCl and 0.10 m CaCl2, assuming ideal dissociation.
  5. Describe qualitatively what happens when a cell is placed in a solution that is (a) hypertonic, (b) hypotonic relative to the cell interior.
