Chapter 7: Periodic Properties of the Elements

Learning Objectives

By the end of this tutorial, you should be able to:

  • Explain how electron configurations lead to periodic trends.
  • Describe and compare trends in atomic radius, ionization energy, and electron affinity.
  • Distinguish metallic, nonmetallic, and metalloid behavior.
  • Relate periodic trends to simple chemical reactivity patterns.

Key Vocabulary

  • Effective nuclear charge (Zeff): net positive charge experienced by valence electrons.
  • Atomic radius: size of an atom, often measured as half the distance between bonded nuclei.
  • Ionization energy (IE): energy required to remove an electron from a gaseous atom or ion.
  • Electron affinity (EA): energy change when an atom gains an electron.
  • Isoelectronic series: ions/atoms with the same number of electrons.
  • Metal: element that is typically shiny, malleable, and conducts heat and electricity.
  • Nonmetal: element that is often brittle (if solid) and a poor conductor.

1. Effective Nuclear Charge and Shielding

  • Core electrons shield valence electrons from the full nuclear charge.
  • Zeff increases across a period, pulling electrons closer to the nucleus.

2. Periodic Trends

  • Atomic radius: decreases left → right across a period; increases down a group.
  • Ionization energy: increases left → right; decreases down a group (with some exceptions).
  • Electron affinity: often becomes more negative across a period (stronger attraction for extra electrons).
  • Metallic character: increases down a group, decreases across a period.

3. Reactivity Patterns

  • Alkali metals (Group 1) are very reactive metals; they form M+ and react vigorously with water.
  • Halogens (Group 17) are reactive nonmetals; they form X− and act as strong oxidizing agents.
  • Noble gases (Group 18) are mostly unreactive due to filled valence shells.

4. Practice Problems (Self-Check)

  1. Arrange the following in order of increasing atomic radius: N, O, C, F.
  2. Which atom in each pair has the higher first ionization energy? (a) Na or K; (b) Mg or Al; (c) Cl or Ar.
  3. Rank the ions O2−, F−, Na+, Mg2+ in order of increasing radius. Explain your reasoning briefly.
  4. Explain why metals tend to form cations while nonmetals tend to form anions, using periodic trends.
  5. Predict whether the reaction 2 Na(s) + Cl2(g) → 2 NaCl(s) is exothermic or endothermic overall and relate your answer to periodic trends and stability.
