Chapter 18: Chemistry of the Environment

Learning Objectives

By the end of this tutorial, you should be able to:

  • Describe key chemical processes in the atmosphere, including ozone formation and depletion.
  • Explain the greenhouse effect and identify major greenhouse gases and their sources.
  • Outline basic chemistry of acid rain and discuss impacts on ecosystems and materials.

Key Vocabulary

  • Troposphere: lowest layer of the atmosphere, where weather occurs.
  • Stratosphere: layer above the troposphere, containing the ozone layer.
  • Ozone (O3): allotrope of oxygen that absorbs UV radiation in the stratosphere.
  • Greenhouse effect: warming of Earth due to gases that absorb outgoing infrared (IR) radiation.
  • Greenhouse gas: gas that absorbs IR (for example, CO2, H2O vapor, CH4, N2O).
  • Acid rain: precipitation with pH < 5.6 due to dissolved acidic oxides such as SO2 and NOx.

1. Atmospheric Chemistry and Ozone

  • Ozone in the stratosphere forms and decomposes via photochemical reactions with UV light.
  • This ozone layer absorbs harmful UV‑B and UV‑C radiation, protecting living organisms.
  • Certain chlorine‑containing compounds (for example, CFCs) can catalytically destroy ozone, thinning the ozone layer.

2. Greenhouse Effect and Climate

  • Sunlight (short‑wavelength) passes through the atmosphere and warms Earth’s surface.
  • Earth re‑radiates energy as longer‑wavelength IR, which greenhouse gases absorb and re‑emit, trapping heat.
  • Increasing greenhouse gas concentrations enhance this effect and shift Earth’s energy balance.

3. Acid Rain and Water Chemistry

  • Combustion of sulfur‑containing fuels forms SO2, which can be oxidized and hydrated to form H2SO4.
  • High‑temperature combustion (for example, in engines) produces NO and NO2, which can form nitric acid.
  • Acidic deposition lowers pH of lakes and soils, damages forests, and accelerates corrosion of metals and stone.

4. Practice Problems (Self-Check)

  1. Explain qualitatively why ozone in the stratosphere is beneficial, but ozone near Earth’s surface can be a pollutant.
  2. Name three major greenhouse gases and describe one major source for each.
  3. Write symbolic steps showing how SO2 from coal burning can contribute to acid rain (formation of H2SO4).
  4. Describe one strategy for reducing emissions of (a) SO2, (b) NOx, or (c) CO2, and briefly discuss trade‑offs.
