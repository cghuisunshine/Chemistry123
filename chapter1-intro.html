Chapter 1: Introduction, Matter, Energy, and Measurement

Learning Objectives

By the end of this tutorial, you should be able to:

  • Classify matter as element, compound, or mixture; solid, liquid, or gas.
  • Distinguish physical vs. chemical properties and changes.
  • Use SI units, prefixes, and significant figures in calculations.
  • Convert units with dimensional analysis and interpret density.

Key Vocabulary

  • Matter: anything that has mass and occupies space.
  • Element: a substance that cannot be broken down by chemical means.
  • Compound: a substance composed of two or more elements in fixed ratios.
  • Mixture: a physical combination of substances (homogeneous or heterogeneous).
  • Physical change: alters form but not composition (for example, melting ice).
  • Chemical change: transforms one substance into another (for example, rusting iron).
  • SI unit: standard unit such as meter (m), kilogram (kg), second (s), kelvin (K).
  • Significant figures: digits in a measured number that carry meaning about precision.
  • Density: mass per unit volume, \rho = m/V.

1. Classification of Matter

  • States: solids (fixed shape/volume), liquids (variable shape, fixed volume), gases (variable shape and volume).
  • Composition:
    • Pure substances: elements and compounds.
    • Mixtures: homogeneous (uniform; for example, saltwater) and heterogeneous (non-uniform; for example, sand and water).

2. Units, Prefixes, and Measurements

  • Common prefixes: kilo (103), centi (10−2), milli (10−3), micro (10−6).
  • Temperature scales: Celsius, Kelvin; use K = °C + 273.15.
  • Use dimensional analysis: multiply by conversion factors so units cancel.

3. Significant Figures and Calculations

  • Nonzero digits are always significant; zeros between nonzeros are significant.
  • Leading zeros are not significant; trailing zeros may be, depending on decimal point.
  • Add/subtract: match the fewest decimal places; multiply/divide: match the fewest significant figures.

4. Practice Problems (Self-Check)

  1. Classify each as element, compound, homogeneous mixture, or heterogeneous mixture: air, NaCl(s), brass, granite.
  2. Identify each as a physical or chemical change: boiling water, burning paper, dissolving sugar, tarnishing silver.
  3. A metal sample has mass 12.8 g and volume 4.00 mL. Calculate its density and express your answer with the correct number of significant figures.
  4. Convert 6.50 × 102 mg to grams and kilograms using dimensional analysis.
  5. A temperature is 37.0 °C. Express this in kelvin and in degrees Fahrenheit.
