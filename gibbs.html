Gibbs Free Energy as the Second Law (Constant T, P)

Big picture

Key ideas

  • The Second Law gives the direction of change: the universe tends to move toward higher total entropy.
  • Gibbs free energy is not a “new law”; it repackages the Second Law into a system-only test for spontaneity when T and P are constant and only PV work occurs.
  • The trick is to rewrite the surroundings entropy change in terms of measurable energy changes of the system.

Important formulas & their meaning

  • Second Law: ΔS_univ = ΔS_sys + ΔS_surr ≥ 0. “Universe” means system + surroundings; spontaneous change makes the sum nonnegative.

Step 1 — Make the surroundings computable (thermal reservoir)

Key ideas

  • Treat the surroundings as a huge heat bath at constant temperature T (a thermal reservoir).
  • Because entropy is a state function, you can use a reversible heat transfer to compute entropy changes even if the real process is irreversible.

Important formulas & their meaning

  • Entropy definition: ΔS = ∫(δq_rev/T). Entropy change depends on reversible heat flow divided by temperature.
  • Reservoir entropy change: at constant T, ΔS_surr = q_surr/T.
  • Heat balance: q_surr = −q_sys, so ΔS_surr = −q_sys/T.

Short example(s)

Interpretation: if the system releases heat (q_sys < 0), the surroundings gain heat and ΔS_surr increases.

Step 2 — Replace heat with enthalpy at constant pressure (qp = ΔH)

Key ideas

  • Most chemistry happens at (approximately) constant pressure, often with only PV work.
  • Under constant pressure and PV-only work, the heat exchanged by the system equals the enthalpy change.

Important formulas & their meaning

  • First Law: ΔU = q + w, where ΔU tracks internal energy, q is heat into the system, and w is work done on the system.
  • PV work: w = −P_ext ΔV. Expansion (ΔV > 0) means the system does work on the surroundings, so w is negative.
  • Constant-pressure heat: with P_ext = P, ΔU = q_p − PΔV, so q_p = ΔU + PΔV.
  • Enthalpy definition: H ≡ U + PV, so at constant P, ΔH = ΔU + PΔV.
  • Key result: comparing the last two lines gives q_p = ΔH.

Short example(s)

Interpretation: at constant pressure, heat flow includes both changing U and supplying energy for expansion work; H packages those together.

Step 3 — Substitute into the Second Law

Key ideas

  • Use Step 1 to write ΔS_surr in terms of the system’s heat flow.
  • Use Step 2 to replace that heat flow with the system’s enthalpy change at constant pressure.

Important formulas & their meaning

  • From Step 1: ΔS_surr = −q_sys/T.
  • From Step 2: at constant pressure, q_sys = q_p = ΔH_sys.
  • So: ΔS_surr = −ΔH_sys/T.
  • Second Law rewritten: ΔS_univ = ΔS_sys − (ΔH_sys/T).
  • Multiply by −T (T > 0): −TΔS_univ = ΔH_sys − TΔS_sys.

How temperature T is related to entropy S

Key ideas

  • In thermodynamics, temperature can be defined through how energy changes with entropy.
  • This is why the product TS appears naturally in Gibbs free energy.

Important formulas & their meaning

  • Fundamental definition: 1/T ≡ (∂S/∂E)_{V,N}, equivalently T ≡ (∂E/∂S)_{V,N}.
  • Reversible energy–entropy relation: dS = dE/T.
  • Thermal equilibrium: T_A = T_B ⇔ (∂S/∂E)_A = (∂S/∂E)_B.

Step 4 — Gibbs free energy emerges

Key ideas

  • Define a thermodynamic potential that matches ΔH − TΔS under constant temperature.
  • This makes spontaneity testable using system properties only (under the stated conditions).

Important formulas & their meaning

  • Definition: G ≡ H − TS.
  • At constant T: ΔG = ΔH − TΔS.
  • Core identity (constant T, P; PV work only): ΔG = −TΔS_univ.

Spontaneity criterion (why chemists love ΔG)

Key ideas

  • Because T > 0, the sign of ΔG is the opposite of the sign of ΔS_univ.
  • This is why “ΔG < 0” is a convenient spontaneity test at constant T and P.

Important formulas & their meaning

  • Spontaneous: ΔS_univ > 0 ⇔ ΔG < 0.
  • Equilibrium: ΔS_univ = 0 ⇔ ΔG = 0.
  • Non-spontaneous: ΔS_univ < 0 ⇔ ΔG > 0.

When the shortcut needs care (boundaries of the derivation)

Key ideas

  • If the assumptions break, go back to the Second Law ΔS_univ ≥ 0 and rebuild with the correct constraints.

Rules/conditions

  • Not constant pressure: you can’t replace q by ΔH so simply.
  • Non-PV work present (electrical/shaft/etc.): the bookkeeping changes; the neat “only ΔH” step needs modification.
  • Surroundings not a reservoir / T not constant: ΔS_surr = q_surr/T with a single T may fail.

One-line exam summary

Memorize: at constant T and P (PV work only), ΔG = −TΔS_univ, so ΔG < 0 means spontaneous.

Symbols: G Gibbs free energy, H enthalpy, S entropy, U internal energy, q heat, w work, P pressure, V volume, T temperature.
