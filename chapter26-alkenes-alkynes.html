Chapter 26: Chemistry of Alkenes and Alkynes

Learning Objectives

By the end of this tutorial, you should be able to:

  • Recognize and name simple alkenes and alkynes.
  • Describe π bonding and its consequences for geometry and restricted rotation.
  • Outline key addition reactions of alkenes and alkynes at a conceptual level.

Key Vocabulary

  • Alkene: hydrocarbon with C=C double bond(s).
  • Alkyne: hydrocarbon with C≡C triple bond(s).
  • π bond: side‑by‑side overlap of p orbitals; present in double and triple bonds.
  • Addition reaction: reagents add across multiple bonds, reducing overall π bond count.

1. Structure and Geometry

  • Carbon atoms in C=C are sp2 hybridized (trigonal planar, ~120°); in C≡C they are sp (linear, 180°).
  • Restricted rotation around C=C can lead to cis/trans (E/Z) isomerism.

2. Conceptual Reactivity

  • Electron‑rich π bonds act as nucleophiles toward electrophiles.
  • General pattern: electrophile adds to one carbon of the double bond, nucleophile adds to the other, often following Markovnikov‑type rules (details in later chapters).

3. Practice Problems (Self-Check)

  1. Draw line‑angle structures for propene, 1‑butene, 2‑butene (cis and trans), and propyne.
  2. Explain why rotation around a C=C bond is restricted, but rotation around a C–C single bond is not, in terms of σ and π bonding.
  3. For a simple addition (such as HBr adding to propene), describe in words the overall change in bonding (number of σ and π bonds before and after).
