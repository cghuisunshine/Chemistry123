Chapter 15: Chemical Equilibrium

Learning Objectives

By the end of this tutorial, you should be able to:

  • Write equilibrium constant expressions in terms of concentration (Kc) and pressure (Kp).
  • Relate the reaction quotient Q to K and predict the direction of shift.
  • Set up and solve ICE tables for equilibrium concentrations.
  • Apply Le Châtelier’s principle to predict how systems respond to disturbances.

Key Vocabulary

  • Equilibrium: dynamic state where forward and reverse reaction rates are equal.
  • Equilibrium constant (K): ratio of product to reactant activities at equilibrium.
  • Reaction quotient (Q): same expression as K but for any set of concentrations or pressures.
  • Le Châtelier’s principle: system at equilibrium shifts to counteract an imposed change.

1. Writing K and Q

  • For aA + bB ⇌ cC + dD, K_c = [C]^c[D]^d / ([A]^a[B]^b) (omit solids and pure liquids).
  • For gases, Kp uses partial pressures; Kp and Kc are related by K_p = K_c(RT)^Δn.

2. Using Q to Predict Direction

  • Q < K: system has too much reactant; shifts right (toward products).
  • Q > K: system has too much product; shifts left (toward reactants).
  • Q = K: system is at equilibrium.

3. ICE Tables and Approximations

  • ICE: Initial, Change, Equilibrium; use balanced equation to relate changes.
  • For small x approximations, justify that x ≪ initial when K is very small or very large.

4. Le Châtelier’s Principle

  • Adding reactant or removing product shifts equilibrium right; the opposite shifts left.
  • Increasing pressure (decreasing volume) favors the side with fewer moles of gas.
  • Temperature changes effectively change K; for exothermic reactions, increasing T shifts equilibrium left.

5. Practice Problems (Self-Check)

  1. Write the equilibrium expression for: N2O4(g) ⇌ 2 NO2(g).
  2. For an equilibrium mixture with [A] = 0.20 M, [B] = 0.10 M, [C] = 0.40 M, [D] = 0.50 M and reaction aA + bB ⇌ cC + dD, calculate Q and compare to a given K.
  3. Set up an ICE table for the dissociation of acetic acid (weak acid) HA ⇌ H+ + A− starting from 0.10 M HA and 0 added H+ or A−.
  4. For N2(g) + 3 H2(g) ⇌ 2 NH3(g), predict qualitatively how increasing pressure or decreasing temperature affects the equilibrium amount of NH3.
