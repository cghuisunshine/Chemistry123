Chapter 20: Electrochemistry

Learning Objectives

By the end of this tutorial, you should be able to:

  • Describe oxidation and reduction and assign oxidation numbers.
  • Balance redox equations using the half‑reaction method.
  • Interpret galvanic (voltaic) and electrolytic cells, including anode, cathode, and electron flow.
  • Calculate standard cell potentials and relate E° to ΔG° and K.

Key Vocabulary

  • Oxidation: loss of electrons; oxidation state increases.
  • Reduction: gain of electrons; oxidation state decreases.
  • Anode: electrode where oxidation occurs.
  • Cathode: electrode where reduction occurs.
  • Cell potential (E): driving force for electron flow.
  • Standard cell potential (E°): cell potential under standard conditions.
  • Nernst equation: relates E to E°, temperature, and reaction quotient Q.

1. Oxidation Numbers and Redox

  • Assign oxidation states using standard rules (elements = 0, sum equals charge, oxygen usually −2, etc.).
  • A species is oxidized if its oxidation state increases; reduced if it decreases.

2. Galvanic Cells and Cell Notation

  • Electron flow: anode → cathode through external circuit.
  • Cell notation: anode | anode solution ‖ cathode solution | cathode.
  • E°cell = E°cathode − E°anode.

3. E°, ΔG°, and K

  • ΔG° = −nFE° (n = moles of electrons; F = Faraday constant).
  • E° = (0.0592 V / n) log K at 25 °C (for base‑10 log).

4. Practice Problems (Self-Check)

  1. Assign oxidation numbers to all atoms in: KMnO4, H2SO4, and NO3−.
  2. Balance the redox reaction in acidic solution: Fe2+ + Cr2O72− → Fe3+ + Cr3+.
  3. Sketch a galvanic cell made from Zn(s)/Zn2+ and Cu(s)/Cu2+. Label anode, cathode, and direction of electron flow.
  4. Using tabulated E° values, calculate E° for the Zn–Cu cell and determine whether the reaction is spontaneous.
