Chapter 20: Electrochemistry

Learning Objectives

By the end of this tutorial, you should be able to:

  • Assign oxidation numbers and identify oxidation and reduction in chemical reactions.
  • Balance redox equations using the half-reaction method (conceptually).
  • Describe galvanic (voltaic) and electrolytic cells, including anode, cathode, and direction of electron flow.
  • Calculate standard cell potentials and relate E° to ΔG° and K qualitatively.

Key Vocabulary

  • Oxidation: loss of electrons; oxidation state increases.
  • Reduction: gain of electrons; oxidation state decreases.
  • Oxidizing agent: species that causes oxidation and is reduced itself.
  • Reducing agent: species that causes reduction and is oxidized itself.
  • Anode: electrode where oxidation occurs.
  • Cathode: electrode where reduction occurs.
  • Cell potential (E): driving force for electron flow; measured in volts (V).
  • Standard cell potential (E°): cell potential under standard conditions.

1. Oxidation Numbers and Redox

  • Assign oxidation states using rules (elements = 0; monatomic ions = charge; oxygen usually −2; hydrogen +1 with nonmetals; sum equals overall charge).
  • Species whose oxidation state increases is oxidized; whose oxidation state decreases is reduced.

Worked Example 1 – Oxidation States

Assign oxidation numbers to all atoms in: KMnO4, H2SO4, and NO3−.

  1. KMnO4: K = +1, O = −2 → Mn must be +7 (so total = 0).
  2. H2SO4: H = +1, O = −2 → 2(+1) + S + 4(−2) = 0 → S = +6.
  3. NO3−: O = −2 → N + 3(−2) = −1 → N = +5.

2. Galvanic Cells and Cell Notation

  • Galvanic (voltaic) cell: spontaneous redox reaction that produces a current.
  • Electron flow: anode → cathode through external circuit.
  • Salt bridge maintains charge neutrality by allowing ion flow.

Cell Notation

Example: Zn(s) | Zn2+(aq) || Cu2+(aq) | Cu(s)

  • Left side is anode (oxidation): Zn(s) → Zn2+ + 2 e−.
  • Right side is cathode (reduction): Cu2+ + 2 e− → Cu(s).

3. E°, ΔG°, and K (Qualitative)

  • Standard cell potential: E°cell = E°cathode − E°anode.
  • Relationship to free energy: ΔG° = −nFE°cell (n = moles of electrons; F = Faraday constant).
  • If E°cell > 0, ΔG° < 0 → reaction is spontaneous under standard conditions.
  • Relationship to equilibrium constant K: E°cell = (0.0592 V / n) log K at 25 °C (base-10 log).

4. Practice Problems (Self-Check)

  1. Assign oxidation numbers to all atoms in: HNO3, SO3, Cr2O72−.
  2. Sketch a galvanic cell made from Zn(s)/Zn2+ and Cu(s)/Cu2+. Label the anode, cathode, and direction of electron flow.
  3. Using tabulated E° values (from your text), calculate E° for the Zn–Cu cell and determine whether the reaction is spontaneous as written.
  4. Qualitatively explain how E° and ΔG° help you predict the size of K for a redox reaction.
